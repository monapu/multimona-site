Getting started

MultiMona is designed to be as easy to use as possible. It is intended for domestic (or small scale merchant) users who wish to use a desktop computer for their day to day Monacoin transactions.

This guide will provide you with the first steps you should take after you have installed MultiMona so that you can be confident about how to get things done safely.

Step 1 - Password protect your wallet

When you first open MultiMona a new, empty wallet is created for you called "multimona.wallet". In order to get you started quickly, this wallet will not have a password so it will not be encrypted.

You should now password protect your wallet.

Step 2 - Locate your wallet files

MultiMona is very careful to maintain backups of wallets and as such it is always useful to know where your wallets are being stored.

You can always see the file location of your current wallet in the main title bar of your MultiMona window. For instance, it might say: "MultiMona - My wallet - /Users/jim/Library/Application Support/MultiMona/multimona.wallet". The file name at the end is where your wallet is stored.

Step 3 - Create a new address to receive

Everyone likes to receive monacoin and MultiMona makes this simple.

The receiving monacoin article explains how to generate new receiving addresses and how to keep track of them.

Step 4 - Send some monacoin

Spending monacoin on goods and services helps to keep the Monacoin economy growing. The first time you decide to send some monacoin can be a little daunting so it is worth having a practice with a small amount beforehand.

The sending monacoin article explains how sending monacoin to someone is done.

Finish

That's all there is to it. If you encounter any problems, the main help contents provide answers to many common questions and you should have a look there first.
